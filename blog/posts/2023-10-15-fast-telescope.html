First Experience with the FAST Telescope

October 15, 2023 Reading time: 8 minutes

Last month I was fortunate to receive observing time on the FAST telescope - my first time using the world's largest single-dish radio telescope. This article documents the entire process from proposal preparation to data acquisition, as well as preliminary data analysis results.

The FAST (Five-hundred-meter Aperture Spherical Telescope) is located in Guizhou, China, and has been operational since 2016. With its massive 500-meter diameter, it's particularly sensitive to faint radio signals from pulsars and neutral hydrogen in distant galaxies.

Proposal Preparation

The proposal process was rigorous, requiring detailed justification of the scientific objectives and technical feasibility. I spent several weeks preparing the observing plan, including target selection, integration time calculations, and data reduction strategies.

On-site Experience

Visiting the FAST site was awe-inspiring. The telescope's scale is difficult to comprehend until you see it in person. The support staff were incredibly knowledgeable and helpful throughout the observing run.

Preliminary Results

The data quality exceeded expectations. We detected several new pulsar candidates and gathered valuable data on neutral hydrogen distribution in our target galaxy cluster. Analysis is ongoing, but the initial results are promising.
